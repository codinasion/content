description: "The Liverpool Women's Suffrage Society was set up in 1894 by Edith Bright, Lydia Allen Booth and Nessie Stewart-Brown to promote the Enfranchisement of Women. The Society held its first Meeting in a Temperance Hall in Liverpool with millicent Fawcett the Head of the national Union of Women's Suffrage societies as its Guest Speaker. The Society established its Headquarters in lord Street. The Group became affiliated with the Nuwss in 1898, it held Meetings in Caf√©s which included Talks, Poetry and Dance Recitals. Members were recruited from prominent Members of the Society and they distanced themselves from the working Class Suffrage Societies such as the Women's social and political Union."
