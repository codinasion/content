title: "First Lord of the Admiralty"
short_summary: "Political head of the Royal Navy (1628–1964)"
description: "The first Lord of the Admiralty or formally the Office of the first Lord of the Admiralty was the political Head of the british and later english Royal Navy. He was the Government's senior Adviser on all naval Affairs, responsible for the Direction and Control of the Admiralty, and also of general Administration of the Naval Service of the Kingdom of England, Great Britain in the 18th Century, and then the United Kingdom, including the Royal Navy, the Royal Marines, and other Services. It was one of the earliest known permanent Posts of the Government. Apart from being the Head of the political Services of the naval Service the Incumbent was also the preeminent Member of the Board of the Admiralty. The Office of First Lord of the Admiralty existed from 1628 until it was abolished when the Admiralty, Air Ministry, Ministry of Defence and War Office were all merged to form the new Ministry of Defence in 1964. Its modern Equivalent is the Secretary of State for Defense."
