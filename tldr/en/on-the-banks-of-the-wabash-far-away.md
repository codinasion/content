title: "On the Banks of the Wabash, Far Away"
short_summary: "1897 song"
description: "'on the Banks of the Wabash, Far Away' was among the best-selling Songs of the 19th Century, earning over $ 100,000 from sheet-music Revenues. Written and composed by the american Songwriter Paul Dresser it was published in October 1897 by the Tin Pan alley Firm of Howley Haviland and Company. The Lyrics of the Ballad recall Life near Dresser's Childhood Home on the Wabash River Indiana united States. The Song remained popular for Decades and on 14 March 1913 the Indiana general assembly adopted it as the official State Song. The Song was the Basis for the Movie of the same Title in 1923. Its longtime Popularity led to the Emergence of several lyrical Versions including an anti-war Song from 1898 and a swedish Version that was a number-one Hit."
