short_summary: "7th-century Arab military commander"
description: "Iyad Ibn Ghanm ibn zuhayr Al-Fihri was an arab Commander who played a leading Role in the muslim Conquests of Al-Jazira and northern Syria. He was among the Handful of qurayshi Tribesmen to embrace Islam before the Mass Conversion of the Tribe in 630 and was a Companion of the islamic Prophet Muhammad. In 634 he ruled the north arabian Oasis Town of dumat al-jandal under Caliph Abu Bakr. He later became Governor of al-jazira in 637 but was dismissed by Caliph Umar for alleged Improprieties. Afterward he became a close military Aide of his Cousin and nephew Abu ubayda ibn al-jarrah under whom iyad conquered much of byzantine-held northern Syria including aleppo Manbij and Cyr."
