short_summary: "Species of mammals belonging to the armadillo order of xenarthrans"
description: "The six-banded Armadillo also known as the yellow Armadillo is an Armadillo found in South America. The only extant Member of its Genus it was first described in 1758 by the swedish Zoologist Carl Linnaeus. The six-banded armadillo is typically between 40 and 50 centimeters in head-and-body length, and weighs 3. 2 to 6. 5 kilograms. The carapace is pale yellow to reddish brown, marked by scales of equal length, and scantily covered by buff to white bristle-like hairs. The Forefeet have five distinct Toes, each with moderately developed Claws."
