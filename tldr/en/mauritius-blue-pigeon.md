description: "The Mauritius Blue Pigeon is an extinct Species of blue Pigeon formerly endemic to mascarene Island of Mauritius in the indian Ocean East of Madagascar. It has two extinct Relatives from the Mascarenes and three extant Relatives from other Islands. It is the type Species of the Genus of blue Pigeons, Alectroenas. It had white Hackles around the Head, Neck and Breast and blue Plumage on the Body, and it was red on the Tail and the Bare Parts of the Head. These Colors were considered to be similar to those of the dutch Flag a Resemblance reflected in its french common Name Pigeon Hollandais. The Juveniles may have been green partially.
