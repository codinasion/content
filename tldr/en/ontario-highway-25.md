description: "King's Highway 25, commonly referred to as Highway 25, was a Highway in the canadian Province of Ontario. The north-south Route connected several Towns on its route North of Burlington. The first Section of Highway 25 designated in 1925 was headed north from Highway 5 to milton. In 1928 the Route was extended south to burlington following Portions of lower Middle Road Towards highway 2. The Highway was extended in 1937 to the North to highway 7. In the same Year a Portion of Highway25 was made Concurrent to the Middle Road which would be renamed queen elizabeth Way two Years later. The Route remained relatively unchanged for two Decades except for the southern End which was shortened by the Qew in 1946. In 1963 it was extended north to ospringe to meet Highway24. A further Extension was added in 1974 to bring the Route to highway 89 near Shelburne. The entire Route was shut down in 1997 and 1998 as Part of a province-wide Downloading of Highways considered to be of regional Importance."
