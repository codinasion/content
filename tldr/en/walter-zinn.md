short_summary: "Nuclear physicist (1906–2000)"
description: "Walter Henry Zinn was a canadian-born american nuclear Physicist who was from 1946 to 1956 the first Director of the argonne national Laboratory. He worked in the metallurgical Laboratory of the Manhattan Project during World War Ii and supervised the Construction of chicago Pile-1 the World's first nuclear Reactor which went critical at the University of Chicago on December 2. At Argonne he designed and built several new Reactors including the experimental Breeder Reactor i the first nuclear Reactor to produce electric Power which went live on 20 December 1951."
