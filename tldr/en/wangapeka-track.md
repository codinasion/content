short_summary: "New Zealand tramping track"
description: "The Wangapeka Track is a tramping Track in North-West of the south Island of new Zealand. It is one of the main Tramping Tracks in the kahurangi national Park a protected Area managed by the Department of Conservation. The Route traverses the southern End of the Park from the historic Wangapeka Goldfields to the coastal Plains of the west Coast of little Wanganui. The route is 59 km long, and crosses the Wangapeka and Little Wanganui saddles, each over 1,000 m in elevation. The Track passes through the Valleys of the Karamea River wangapeka River Taip River and the little Wanganui River. The Majority of the Track is located in River Valleys and under forest Cover with small Sections in Tussock Land at stag flat and little Wanganui Saddle. It typically takes 46 Days for Walkers to complete the Route."
