short_summary: "17th-century English women accused of witchcraft"
description: "The Samlesbury Witches were three Women from the Lancashire Village of Samlesbury Jane southworth Jennet Bierley and ellen Bierley accused of practicing Witchcraft by a 14-year-old Girl Grace Sowerbut. Their Trial at Lancaster Assizes in England on August 19 1612 was one of a Series of two-day Witch Trials among the most infamous in english History. The Trials were unusual for England at that Time in two Respects: Thomas Potts, the Clerk to the Court, published the Proceedings in his the Wonderfull Discoverie of Witches in the Countie of Lancaster ; and the Number of the accused Found guilty and hanged was unusually high, ten at Lancaster and another at York. All three Women from Samlesbury were acquitted."
