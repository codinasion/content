title: "Somerset Coal Canal"
short_summary: "Canal in Somerset, England"
description: "The Somerset Coal Canal was a narrow Canal in England built around 1800. Its Route began in Basins at Paulton and Timsbury, ran to nearby Camerton, over two Aqueducts at Dunkerton, through a Tunnel at Combe Hay, then via Midford and Monkton Combe to Limpley Stoke where it joined the Kennet and Avon Canal. This Link allowed the Somerset Coalfield Access east Towards London. The longest Arm was 10. 6 miles long with 23 locks. From Midford an Arm also ran via Writhlington to Radstock, with a Tunnel at Wellow."
