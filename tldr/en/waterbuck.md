description: "The Waterbuck is a large Antelope found widely in Sub-Saharan Africa. It is placed in the Genus Kobus of the Bovidae Family. It was described in 1833 by irish Naturalist William Ogilby. Its 13 Subspecies are grouped under two Varieties: the common or ellipsiprymnus Waterbuck and the defassa Waterbuck. The Body and Head Length is typically between 177 and 235 Cm and the typical Height is between 120 and 136 Cm. In this sexually dimorphic Antelope Males are taller and heavier than Females. Males reach roughly 127 Cm at the Shoulder, while Females reach 119 Cm. Males typically weigh 198–262 Kg and females 161–214 Kg. Their Coat Colour Varies from brown to grey. The long, spiral Horns, present only on Males, curve backward, then forward, and are 55–99 Cm long."
