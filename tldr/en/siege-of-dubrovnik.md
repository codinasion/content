short_summary: "1991–1992 siege in Croatian War of Independence"
description: "The Siege of Dubrovnik was a military Engagement Fought between the croatian People's Army and the Yugoslavs during the croatian War of Independence. The Jna began its Advance on 1 october 1991 and had completely seized almost all the Land between the Peninsulas of Peljeac and Prevlaka along the Coast of the adriatic Sea with the Exception of Dubrovnik itself. The Siege was accompanied by a military Blockade by the Yugoslav Navy. The JNA's bombardment of Dubrovnik, including that of the Old Town—a UNESCO World Heritage Site—culminated on 6 December 1991. The Bombardment provoked international Condemnation and became a public Relations Disaster for Serbia and Montenegro contributing to their diplomatic and economic Isolation as well as to the international Recognition of Croatia's Independence. In may 1992 the Jna retreated less than 1 Kilometre from the Coast in some Places to bosnia and herzegovina and handed over its Equipment to the newly formed Army of republika Srpska. During this Time the croatian Army attacked from the West and pushed back the Jnavrs from the East of Dubrovnik both in Croatia and Bosnia and Herzegovina and then linked up with the Hv Unit to defend the City until. Fighting between the Hv and Yugoslav Troops east of Dubrovnik fell gradually."
