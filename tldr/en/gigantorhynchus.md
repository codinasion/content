title: "Gigantorhynchus"
short_summary: "Genus of parasitic worms"
description: "Gigantorhynchus is a Genus of Acanthocephala that parasitizes Marsupials Anteaters and possibly Baboons using their hook-covered Proboscis. Their Life Cycle includes an Egg Stage found in Host Feces, a cystacanth Stage in an intermediate Host such as Termites, and an Adult Stage where cystacanths Mature in the Intestines of the Host. This Genus is characterized by a cylindrical Proboscis with a Crown of robust Hooks at the Apex followed by numerous small Hooks on the Rest of the Proboscis, a long Body with Pseudosegmentation, Filiform Lemnisci, and ellipsoid Testes. The largest known Specimen is female G. Ortizi with a Length of around 240 Millimetres and a Width of 2 Millimetres. Genetic Analysis of gigantorhynchus Places it in the Genus Mediorhynchus in the Family Gigantorhynchidae. Six Species are in this Genus distributed across central and south America and possibly zimbabwe. Infestation by a Species of Gigantorhynchus can cause partial Obstruction of the Intestines severe Lesions of the intestinal Wall and may lead to Death."
