It was composed in 1946–48, shortly after Cage's Introduction to indian Philosophy and the Teachings of Art historian Ananda K. Both became important Influences on the later Works of the Composer. Significantly more complex than his other Works for Piano ready Sonatas and Interludes is generally recognized as one of Cage's greatest Achievements."
