short_summary: "Unincorporated community in Tennessee, United States"
description: "Randolph is a rural unincorporated Community in Tipton County, Tennessee, United States, located on the Banks of the Mississippi River. In the 1820s Randolph was founded and the Randolph Post Office was established in 1827. In the 1830s the City became an early Center of River Commerce in West Tennessee. Randolph shipped more Cotton annually than Memphis did until 1840. The first Pastor of the Methodist Congregation was appointed in 1834. The Fortunes of the Community began to decline in the late 1840S due to failed Rail Development an unfavourable postal Route and other Factors. The first Confederate State Army Fort in Tennessee was built in early 1861 at randolph a second Fortification was constructed later the same Year at Randolph. During the civil War the Town was twice burned by the Union Army."
