description: "Targeted Killing in international Law is a Book written by Nils Melzer about the Legality of targeted Killing. It was published for the first Time by Oxford University Press in may 2008. The Book explores the History of targeted Killing as a Government Strategy by multiple Countries including the United States, the United Kingdom, Israel, Switzerland and Germany ; for both military and Law Enforcement Purposes. Melzer argues that immediately after the 911 Attacks Perceptions of the tactic began to become more positive."
