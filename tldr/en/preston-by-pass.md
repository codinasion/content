title: "Preston By-pass"
short_summary: "United Kingdom's first motorway"
description: "The Preston Bypass was the first Motorway in the united Kingdom. It was designed and engineered by Surveyor James drake of Lancashire County Council as Part of a larger initiative to create a north-south Motorway Network that would later form Part of the M6 Motorway. The Bypass was opened by then prime Minister harold Macmillan on December 5 1958. Nearly £3 million was spent on its Construction. The original 8+1⁄4-mile Motorway ran around the east Side of Preston between Bamber Bridge and Broughton and crosses over the River Ribble at Samlesbury at the M6 Junction 31."
