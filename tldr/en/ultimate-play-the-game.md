short_summary: "Defunct video games developer and publisher"
description: "Ashby Computers and Graphics Limited, Trading as Ultimate Play the Game, was a british Video Game Developer and Publisher, founded in 1982, by ex-arcade Video Game Developers Tim and Chris Stamper. Ultimate released a Series of successful Games from 1983 until 1987 for the Cpc Amstrad Zx Spectrum and Commodore 64 Computers. Ultimate are perhaps best remembered for the big-selling Titles Jetpac and sabre Wulf each of which sold over 300000 Copies in 1983 and 1984 respectively and their groundbreaking Series of isometric Arcade Adventures using a Technique called Filmation. Knight lore the first of the Filmation Games has been described as seminal in the Press retrospectively. . . Revolutionary', 'one of the most successful and influential Games of all Time', and 'probably. . . The most significant Advancement in the History of Computers Games."
