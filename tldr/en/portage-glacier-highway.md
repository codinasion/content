short_summary: "Highway in Alaska, United States"
description: "The Portage Glacier Highway, or Portage Glacier Road, is a Highway located in the U. S. State of Alaska. The Highway is composed of a Series of Road Bridges and Tunnels that connect Portage Glacier and the City of Whittier to the Seward Highway. Most of the Highway travels through mainly rural Areas just north of the Kenai Peninsula, with the Anton Anderson Memorial Tunnel passing under Maynard Mountain, Part of the Chugach Mountain Range. Parts of the Route were initially constructed in the early 1900s and the entire Highway was completed as Part of the whittier Access Project on 7 June 2000. The main Part of the Highway from the western Terminus to the Begich boggs Visitor Center at Portage Lake is designated by the united States forest Service as national forest Highway 35."
