description: "Delbert allen Rabbit Keen was an american Football back who played two Seasons of professional Football. He played College Football for two Years at Connors agricultural College before spending his last two Years at Arkansas. He was not selected in the 1937 Nfl Draft but managed to remain on the Roster of the Philadelphia eagles nonetheless and appeared in eight Games that Year running for 154 Yards and throwing the second longest Passing of the Season an 86-yard Touchdown against the green Bay Packers - ultimately the only completed Pass of his. In 1938 he split Time between the Eagles and the Richmond Arrows of the Dixie League and did not play later."
