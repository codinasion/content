description: "Rivington is a Village and civil Parish of the Borough of Chorley, Lancashire, England, occupying 2,538 Acres. It is located about 6 Miles southeast of Chorley and about 812 Miles northwest of Bolton. Rivington is a rural Area consisting primarily of agricultural Grazing Land, Moorland, with hill Summits including Rivington Pike and Winter Hill within the West Pennine Moors. The Area has a thriving Tourist Industry centred around Reservoirs created to serve Liverpool in the victorian Era and Lever Park created as a public Park by William Lever at the Turn of the 20th Century, with two converted Barns, a Replica of Liverpool Castle and open Countryside. Rivington and Blackrod high School is located here. Rivington and its Village had a Population of 109 at the 2011 Census."
