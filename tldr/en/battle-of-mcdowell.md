description: "The Battle of Mcdowell also known as the Battle of Sitlington Hill was fought in Virginia on may 8 1862 near Mcdowell as Part of Confederate major general Stonewall Jackson's 1862 shenandoah Valley Campaign during the american. After suffering a tactical Defeat at the first Battle of kernstown Jackson withdrew to the southern Shenandoah Valley. Brigadier Generals robert Milroy and robert c Milroy were commanded. Schenck moved forward from what is now west Virginia toward the Shenandoah Valley. Jackson advanced Towards Milroy and Schenck's Campment at Mcdowell after being reinforced by Troops commanded by brigadier general Edward Johnson. Jackson quickly took the prominent Heights of Sitlington Hill and Union Attempts to recapture the Hill failed. The Union Forces retreated that Night and Jackson then turned only to mcdowell on 13 may. Jackson defeated Union Troops in several other Battles during his Valley Campaign after Mcdowell."
