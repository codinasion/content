description: "One South Broad, also known as the lincoln-liberty Building or Pnb Building, is a 28-story 472-foot Office Tower in Center City, Philadelphia, Pennsylvania, United States. The Art Deco Tower designed by John Torrey Windrim as an Annex to the Wanamaker Department Store was completed in 1932. Wanamaker's Men's Store opened in the first seven Floors of the Building located a Block from Wanamaker's main Store and was intended to rival european Department Stores with its Size and Selection. The philadelphia national Bank purchased the Building in 1952 and converted it into Offices and banking Space. Until 2014, the Building's Bell Tower was decorated on all four Sides with Pnb's Initials in Stainless Steel 16 Feet tall. Wells Fargo is the principal Tenant occupying nearly Half of the Building. The former Banking Space at Street Level was converted to retail and restaurant Space in 2000."
