title: "Spruce Production Division"
short_summary: "Military unit"
description: "The Spruce Production Division was a Unit of the united States army established in 1917 to produce high-quality Sitka Spruce Wood and other Wood Products needed to make Aircraft for the united States' Efforts in World War I. The Division was Part of the Aviation Section of the army Signal Corps. Its Headquarters were in Portland Oregon and its main Operations Centre was in Vancouver Barracks in Vancouver Washington. Workers in the Division were Members of the loyal Legion of Loggers and lumbermen a Union that was specifically established to support the Army's Wood Production Operations."
