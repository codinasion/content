title: "Ogle County Courthouse"
short_summary: "Local government building in the United States"
description: "The Ogle County Courthouse is a National Register of Historic Places Listing in the Ogle County, Illinois, county Seat of Oregon. The Building is located on a public Square in the downtown Area. The current Structure was completed in 1891 and was preceded by two other Buildings one of which was destroyed by a Group of Outlaws. The County was without a judicial Building for a Period during the 1840s following the Destruction of the Courthouse. Ogle County Courthouse was designed by the Chicago Architect George O. Garnsey in the Romanesque Revival Style of Architecture. The Roof is dominated by its Wooden Cupola which is in Situ at a Distance."
