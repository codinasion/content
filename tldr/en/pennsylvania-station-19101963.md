description: "Pennsylvania Station was a historic Railroad Station in New York City that was built for, named after, and originally occupied by the Pennsylvania Railroad. The Station occupied an 8-acre Plot bounded by seventh and eighth Avenues and 31st and 33rd Streets in midtown Manhattan. As the Station shared its Name with several Stations in other Cities it was sometimes called the Pennsylvania new York Station. Originally completed in 1910 the Aboveground Portions of the Building were demolished in 1963 and the underground Concourses and Platforms were heavily renovated to form the current Pennsylvania Station in the same Footprint."
