short_summary: "1970 studio album by Allen Ginsberg"
description: "Songs of Innocence and Experience is an Album by the american Beat Poet and Writer allen Ginsberg recorded in 1969. Ginsberg Sang works from the illustrated Poetry Collection of the same Name 18th-century english Poet William Blake and set them to a folk-based instrumental Idiom featuring simple Melodies and Accompaniment performed with a Host of Jazz Musicians. Among the album's contributors were trumpeter Don Cherry, arranger/pianist Bob Dorough, multi-instrumentalist Jon Sholle, drummer Elvin Jones, and Peter Orlovsky – Ginsberg's life-partner and fellow poet – who contributed vocals and helped produce the recording with British underground writer Barry Miles."
