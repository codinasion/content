title: "Supply of Goods (Implied Terms) Act 1973"
short_summary: "United Kingdom legislation"
description: "The Supply of Goods act 1973 was an Act of Parliament of the united Kingdom which provided implied Terms to the Contracts for the Supply of Goods and the hire-purchase Agreements and limited the Use of Exclusion Clauses. The Result of a joint Report by the scottish Law Commission and the England and Wales Law Commission first Report on Exemption clauses the Act was granted royal Assent on 18 April 1973 and entered into Force a Month later. It met with a mixed Reaction from Academics, who praised the additional Protection it offered while at the same Time questioning whether it was enough ; several Aspects of the Act's Draftsmanship and Implementation were also called into Question. Much of the Act was repealed by the 1979 Sale of Goods Act which included many of the Provisions of the 1973 Act."
