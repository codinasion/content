short_summary: "J. R. R. Tolkien's use of modern literature"
description: "J. R. R. Tolkien derived the Characters, Stories, Places, and Languages of Middle-Earth from many Sources. Among these are several modern Works of Fiction. These include adventure stories from Tolkien's childhood, such as books by John Buchan and H. Rider Haggard, especially the 1887 she: a History of Adventure. Tolkien stated that he used the Battle with Werewolves in Samuel Rutherford Crockett's historical Fantasy the black Douglas in 1899 for his Battle with Wargs."
