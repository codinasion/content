S. 115 was a united States supreme Court Case regarding the Constitutionality of Curfews used during World War Ii when they were applied to the Citizens of the. The case arose out of the implementation of Executive Order 9066 by the U. S. Military to create Zones of Exclusion along the West Coast of the United States, where japanese Americans were subjected to Curfews and eventual Removal to Relocation Centers. This presidential Order followed the Attack on Pearl Harbor that brought america to World War Ii and inflamed the existing anti-japanese Sentiment in the Country."
