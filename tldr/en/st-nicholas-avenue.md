short_summary: "Avenue in Manhattan, New York"
description: "St. Nicholas avenue is a major Street that runs obliquely north-south through several Blocks between 111th and 193rd Streets in the manhattan Borough of new York City. The Route which follows a Course much older than the grid Pattern of the 1811 Commissioners plan passes through the Neighborhoods of harlem Hamilton Heights and Washington Heights. It is believed that it follows the Course of an old indian Trail that was an important Road between the dutch Settlement new Amsterdam and the british new England Colonies in the 17th Century. In the post-colonial Era it became the western End of the Boston Post Road. The Road became a Street when Row Housing in Harlem was being built during its rapid urban Expansion following the End of the american civil War."
