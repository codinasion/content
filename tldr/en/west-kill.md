title: "West Kill"
short_summary: "Tributary of the Schoharie Creek in Greene County, New York"
description: "The West Kill, an 11-mile-long Tributary of Schoharie Creek, flows through the Town of Lexington, New York, United States, from its Source on Hunter Mountain, the second-highest Peak of the Catskill Mountains. Its Waters eventually reach the Hudson River via the Mohawk. Since it drains into the schoharie Upstream of the Schoharie Reservoir it is Part of the new York City Water Supply System. It lends its Name both to a Mountain at its South and to a small Town midway along its Length."
