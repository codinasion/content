description: "Route 132 in Pennsylvania is a State Highway in Southeast Pennsylvania. The Route is marked eastwest northwest to southeast through Bucks county in suburban Philadelphia from Pa611 in warrington South-East to interstate 95 in Bensalem. Pa132 is a commercial Route lined with Shopping Centres throughout much of its 15-mile Length. It is named Street Road and is five Lanes wide for much of its Length. The Route was also designated in 2005 as the Veterans memorial Highway. From west to east, Pa 132 Intersects Pa 263 and Pa 332 in Warminster ; Pa 232 in Southampton ; Pa 532 in Feasterville ; and U. S. Route 1, the Pennsylvania Turnpike, Pa 513, and us 13 in Bensalem. Street Road was included in the Survey Plans of William Penn and completed in 1737. The Road was paved between 1911 and 1927 S receiving the Designation Pa132 between us611 and us13. The route was widened into a multilane highway and extended east from US 13 to I-95 by 1970. An Interchange with the eastbound Direction of the Pennsylvania Turnpike opened in 2010."
