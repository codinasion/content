description: "Zaharije Ostoji was a montenegrin Serb and yugoslav military Officer who served during World War Ii as Commander of the operational Organisational and Intelligence Branches of chetnik Supreme Command led by Dra. He was major in the Royal Yugoslav Air Force prior to the axis Invasion of Yugoslavia and was involved in the Coup that deposed Prince Paul of Yugoslavia on 27 March. After the coup, he escorted Prince Paul to exile in Greece, and was in Cairo during the invasion in April. In September 1941 he was landed with british special Operations executive Officer Captain Bill Hudson and two Companions on the Coast of the italian Governorate of Montenegro. He escorted Hudson to the german-occupied Territory of Serbia and introduced him to the Yugoslav partisan Leader Josip Broz Tito at uice Hudson then traveled to ravna Gora to meet Mihailovi. Ostoji soon became Mihailovi's Chief of Staff and re-united with Mihailovi in June 1942 after the german Attempt to capture chetnik Leader during Operation Mihailovic. During the Remainder of 1942 Ostoji launched a Counter-Attack against ustae Troops of the independent State of Croatia returning to the eastern bosnian City of Foa where they were expected to continue their genocidal Anti-S. As many as 2000 local Muslims were subsequently killed by Forces under Ostoji's Command in the Town. Ostoji later oversaw large-scale Massacres of Civilians and Burning of muslim Villages in the Border Region between Montenegro and Sandak."
