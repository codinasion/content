short_summary: "Geological and biological Site of Special Scientific Interest in Somerset, England"
description: "Crook Peak to Shute Shelve Hill is a 332. 2 hectare geological and biological Site of special scientific Interest located near the western End of the Mendip hills Somerset. The Line of Hills runs about 5 Km from West to east and includes Crook Peak Compton Hill wavering down Cross Plain and Shute Shelve Hill. Most of the Site is owned by the national Trust which bought 725 Acres in 1985 and has much of it designated as common Land. It was notified as an Sssi in 1952 by natural England."
