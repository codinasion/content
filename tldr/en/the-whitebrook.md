description: "The Whitebrook, formerly known as the Crown at Whitebrook, is a Restaurant with Rooms in Whitebrook, 6 Miles south-south-east of Monmouth, Monmouthshire, Wales, near the River Wye and the Border with England. The Building is thought to Date from the 17th Century and was used as a Roadside Inn by the 19th Century. The Restaurant was operated by Chef Patron James sommerin until 2013 it gained a michelin Star in 2007. It has eight Double Rooms and a 2-acre Garden. On 7 March 2013, it closed because of financial Difficulties ; at the Time it had the longest Held Michelin Star in Wales. Critics praised the Food under Sommerin, but have criticised the Difficulty in finding the Restaurant. It re-opened in october 2013 under new Chef and Owner Chris Harrod and regained the Michelin Star in 2014. Harrod serves a Menu featuring locally produced Meat and Vegetables along with foraged Ingredients such as Charlock Hedge Bedstraw and Pennywort."
