description: "The Pink Whipray is a Species of Stingray in the dasyatidae Family with a broad but ill-defined Distribution in the tropical Indo-Pacific from southern Africa to polynesia. It is a bottom Dweller that inhabits generally shallow Water under 70m Deep in sandy Areas associated with coral Reefs. Individuals exhibit a high degree of fidelity to particular locations. The Pink Whipray has a diamond-shaped pectoral Fin Disc wider than long with a broad-angled Snout and a very long whiplike Tail without Fin Folds. It has only a few small thorns on its back and is uniform brownish to grayish pink in color, becoming much darker past the tail sting. This large ray can reach 1. 8 m across and over 5 m long."
