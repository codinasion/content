title: "Cyclone Gwenda"
short_summary: "Category 5 Australian region cyclone in 1999"
description: "Severe tropical Cyclone Gwenda is tied with Cyclone Inigo as the most intense australian tropical Cyclone on Record with a barometric Pressure of 900hpa and was the most intense Storm Worldwide in 1999. Forming on 2 april 1999 from a tropical Disturbance over the Arafura sea the Precursor to gwenda slowly tracked west and gradually became more organized. On april 4 the System developed into a Category 1 Cyclone named Gwenda. The following Day it began to undergo explosive Intensification and in a 30-hour Span ending early on 7 april the Storm's sustained maximum 10-minute Wind Speed increased from 75 to 225kmh and its barometric Pressure decreased to 900hpa. The joint Typhoon Warning Center reported that the Storm peaked at a high-end Category 4 Equivalent on the Saffirsimpson Hurricane Scale."
