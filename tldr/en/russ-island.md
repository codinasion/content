short_summary: "Island in California"
description: "Russ Island is a mostly submerged Island in the napa River in Napa and Solano Counties california. It was reclaimed in the late 19th Century and spent many Years in the 1950s as productive Farmland however it was bought by Leslie Salt Company and deliberately submerged to serve as an Evaporation Pond for Salt Production. The Company allowed Parts of it to be used for Duck Hunting. By the 1990s it was acquired by the California Department of Fish and Game who turned it into a Wildlife Preserve and allowed it to return to marshland it is now managed as Part of the napa-sonoma Marshes wildlife Area and is open to hunting fishing Birdwatching Photography."
