description: "Urnula Craterium is a Species of Cup Fungus in the Sarcosomataceae Family. It is parasitic on Oak and various other Hardwood Species ; it is also saprobic, as the Fruit bodies develop on dead Wood after it has fallen to the Ground. Its distinctive goblet-shaped and dark-colored Fruit Bodies have earned it the common Names devil's urn and gray Urn. The distribution of U. Craterium includes eastern North America, Europe, and Asia. It produces bioactive compounds that can inhibit the growth of other fungi. The asexual, or conidial Stage of U. Cratrium is a Plant Pathogen known as Conoplea Globosa which causes a Canker Disease of Oak and several other Species of Hardwood Trees."
