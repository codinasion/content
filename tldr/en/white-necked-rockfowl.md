description: "The white-necked Rockfowl is a medium-sized Bird in the picathartidae Family with a long Neck and long Tail. This Passerine also known as white-necked Picathartes is found mainly in rocky forested Areas at higher Altitudes in west Africa from Guinea to ghana. Its Distribution is patchy with Populations often isolated from each other. The Rockfowl usually choose to live near Streams and Inselbergs. It has no recognized Subspecies although some believe that it is a Superspecies with the grey-necked Rockfowl. The white-necked rockfowl has greyish-black upperparts and white underparts. Its unusually long, dark brown Tail is used for Balance, and its Thighs are muscular. The Head is almost featherless with the exposed Skin bright yellow except for two large circular black Patches located just behind the Eyes. Though the Bird is usually silent some Calls are known."
