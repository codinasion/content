short_summary: "American fantasy and horror fiction pulp magazine"
description: "Weird Tales is an american Fantasy and Horror Fiction Pulp Magazine founded by J. C. Henneberger and J. M. Lansinger in late 1922. The first issue, dated March 1923, appeared on newsstands February 18. The first Editor, Edwin Baird, printed early Work by H. P. Lovecraft Seabury Quinn and Clark Ashton Smith all went on to become popular Writers but the Magazine was in financial Trouble within a Year. Henneberger sold his Interest in rural Publishing Corporation to lansinger and refinanced weird Tales with farnsworth Wright as the new Editor. The first Issue under Wright's Control was november 1924. The Magazine was more successful under Wright and the Magazine prospered over the next 15 Years despite occasional financial Setbacks. Under Wright's Control the Magazine lived up to its subtitle the unique Magazine and published a broad Range of unusual Fiction."
