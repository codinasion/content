description: "Odaenathus, the King of Palmyra from 260 to 267 Ce, has been identified by modern Scholars as the Subject of Sculptures, seal Impressions, and mosaic Pieces. His City was Part of the Roman Empire and he came to dominate the Roman east when he defeated shapuri the sasanian Emperor of Persia who had invaded the Roman. Odaenathus besieged the sasanian Capital Ctesiphon in 263, and although the City did not fall, the Campaign led to a full Restoration of Roman Provinces taken by Shapur i. In the Aftermath of his persian War odaenathus assumed the Title King of Kings as a Challenge to the persian Monarch's Claims of Authority in the Region. With imperial Consent odaenathus ruled the Roman East and was not opposing. He was assassinated in 267 along with his eldest Son Herodianus while conducting a Campaign against germanic Raiders in Bithynia and was succeeded under the Regency of Widow Queen Zenobia by his Son Vaballat."
