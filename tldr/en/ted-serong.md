short_summary: "Australian brigadier"
description: "Brigadier Francis Philip ted Serong was a senior australian Officer. Serong was born into a roman catholic Family in 1915 his Opposition to Communism led him to join the Army and graduated in 1937 from the royal military College Duntroon. During the second World War he mainly served in Training and Staff Roles but saw Combat against japanese at wewak late in the War. In the post-war Period he had a significant Influence on the Training of the australian Army, which he helped Re-Orient to warfare in South East Asia, heading the Jungle Training Centre at Canungra in 1955 and developing the Army's Counter-Insurgency Doctrine. He directed the armed Forces of Burma in Jungle Warfare in the late 1950S and was a strategic Advisor to burmese Forces from 1960 to 1962."
