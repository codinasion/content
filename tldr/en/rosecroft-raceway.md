short_summary: "Harness racing track in Fort Washington, Maryland, US"
description: "Rosecroft Raceway, nicknamed the 'Raceway by the Beltway' for being close to Interstate 495, is a Harness racing Track in Fort Washington, Maryland. It first opened in 1949 and was owned by William E. Miller, a horse trainer and breeder. Rosecroft quickly became the political and social Center of Prince George's County attracting Thousands of People to each Race Day. In the early 1950s average Attendance was over 7000 a Day. When Miller died in 1954 his Son John owned rosecroft until his Death in 1969. Rosecroft hosted annual memorial Stake Races for both William and John until 1995. Following the Death of John Miller earle Brown controlled Operations until he moved to a different Position in 1980 William E. Miller Ii took over following Brown."
