title: "The Great Artiste"
short_summary: "Aircraft used during the raid on Hiroshima on 6 August 1945"
description: "The Great Artiste was a U. S. Air Force Silverplate b-29 Bomber assigned to the 393d Bomb squadron 509th composite Group. The Aircraft was named for its Bombardier Captain Kermit Beahan in Reference to his Bombing Talents. It flew 12 Training and Practice Missions in which it bombed japanese-held Pacific Islands and dropped pumpkin Bombs on Targets in Japan. It was the only Aircraft to have participated in both the Bombings of Hiroshima and Nagasaki, albeit as an Observation Aircraft on each Mission."
