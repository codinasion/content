short_summary: "Commander of the Royal Australian Air Force (born 1935)"
description: "Air marshal Raymond George Funnell is a retired Commander of the Royal australian Air Force. From 1987 to 1992 he served as Air Force Chief of Staff. He graduated from raaf College and began his Career in the 1950s and 1960s flying Cac Sabre Jet Fighters in Australia and south east Asia. From 1972 to 1975 he commanded No. 6 Squadron during which Time general Dynamics 'f-111c swing-wing Bomber entered australian Service. In the early 1980s he held senior Staff Positions. In 1986 he was promoted to the Position of Air Marshal and became the inaugural Vice-Chief of the Defence Force. Funnell was appointed Cas in July 1987 and was closely involved in the Development and Dissemination of the Doctrine of Air Power. He retired from Raaf in october 1992 following his Term as Cas and was founding Principal of the australian College of Defence and strategic Studies from 1994 to 1998. Since then he has served on various federal Government Committees for Immigration and Detention."
