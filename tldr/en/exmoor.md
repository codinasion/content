It is named after the Exe River which originates in the Centre of the Area about two Miles north-west of Simonsbath. Exmoor is more precisely defined as the Area of the former Ancient Royal hunting forest, also called Exmoor, which was officially surveyed 1815â€“1818 as 18,810 Acres in Extent. The Moor has given its Name to a National Park, which includes the Brendon Hills, the East Lyn Valley, the Vale of Porlock and 55 Km of the Bristol Channel Coast. The total area of the Exmoor National Park is 692. 8 Km2, of which 71 % is in Somerset and 29 % in Devon."
