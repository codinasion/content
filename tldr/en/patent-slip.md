pageid: 6182885
title: "Patent slip"
short_summary: "Mechanism for hauling ships out of water"
description: "A Patent Slip or maritime Railway is an inclined Plane from Shore to Water with a Cradle on which a Ship is floated first and a Mechanism to haul the Ship attached to the Cradle out of the Water onto a Slip. The Maritime Railway was invented in the early 19th Century by a Scot Thomas Morton as a cheaper alternative to dry Docks for the Repairs of Marine Vessels under Water. Larger modern Marine Railways can handle Vessels of Thousands of Tons."
