short_summary: "2012 boat race between Oxford and Cambridge universities"
description: "The 158th Boat Race took Place on April 7 2012. The Boat Race is held annually along the Thames in London and is a side-by-side Rowing Race between Crews from Universities oxford and cambridge. Despite Cambridge having a heavier Crew Oxford were pre-race Favourites having had a successful Preparation Period including a Victory over Leander. Cambridge won the Toss and chose to start on the surrey Side of the River. Partway through the Race the Boat Level was temporarily halted to avoid Injury to protester trenton Oldfield who swam in Front of the two Crews. After the Race was restarted one of the Oxford Crew suffered irreparable Damage to his Blade following a Collision of Oars with the Cambridge Boat ending Oxford's Chances of Victory. The Race was finally won by Cambridge by 414 Lengths in a Time of 17 Minutes 23 Seconds."
