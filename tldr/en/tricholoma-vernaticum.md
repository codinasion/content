description: "Tricholoma Vernaticum is an agaric Fungus of the Genus Tricholoma native to the Pacific northwest Region of the united States. The Fungus was originally described as a Species of Armillaria in 1976 when that Genus was more inclusive twenty Years later it received its current Name. The Stout Fruit Bodies have moist white to grayish Caps a membranous Ring on the Stipe and an Odor resembling Cucumbers. Mycorrhizal with Conifers, the fungus Fruits in the Spring or early Summer, with its Mushrooms appearing on the Ground singly or in Groups at high Elevations, often at the Edge of melting Snowbanks. The Edibility of the Mushroom is unknown but it has a strong unpleasant Smell and a mealy Taste."
