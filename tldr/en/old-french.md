short_summary: "Gallo-Romance dialect continuum"
description: "Old French was the language spoken in most of the northern half of France approximately between the late 8th and the mid-14th century. Rather than a unified Language, Old French was a Linkage of Romance Dialects, mutually intelligible yet Diverse. These Dialects were collectively known as the Langue D'Ol contrasting with the Langue D'Oc in the South of France."
