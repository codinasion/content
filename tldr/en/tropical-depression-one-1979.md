The second tropical Cyclone of the atlantic Hurricane Season the Depression developed in June 11 from a tropical Wave South of grand Cayman. Tracking generally northward the Depression passed west of Jamaica. On June 12, the Depression peaked with Maximum sustained Winds of 35 Mph, never having reached tropical Storm Status. The following Day it reached Cuba where minimal Impacts were recorded. The Depression began leaking into the western atlantic ocean early on June 14 and then continued to the east Coast of Florida for a few Days parallel to the. On June 16 the Depression made another Landfall in south Carolina and dissipated shortly thereafter."
