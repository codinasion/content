description: "Samuel John Everett Loxton was an australian Cricketer, Footballer and Politician. His greatest Achievements among these three Pursuits were attained on the Cricket Field where he played in 12 Tests for Australia from 1948 to 1951. Loxton was Part of Don Bradman's Invincibles who went through the Tour of England in 1948 unbeaten an unprecedented Achievement never matched. As well as being a hard-hitting Middle-Order Batsman, Loxton was a right-arm Fast-Medium swing Bowler who liked to aim at the upper Bodies of the Opposition, and an Outfielder with an Accurate and powerful Throw. Loxton represented Victoria for seven more Seasons after being dropped from the national Team before retiring from First-Class Cricket. He served as an Administrator after his Playing Days were over and spent 24 Years as a liberal Party Member of the victorian Legislative Assembly. Loxton also played in the victorian Football League for St Kilda as a Forward up until 1946. In all three Arenas he was known for his energetic Approach."
