short_summary: "County-class heavy cruiser of the Royal Navy"
description: "Hms Cornwall pennant Number 56 was a heavy Cruiser of the kent Subclass of the County Class built in the Mid-20S for the Royal Navy. The ship Spent most of her pre-world War Ii Career assigned to the China Station. Shortly after the War began in August 1939 she was assigned to search for german Business Raiders in the indian Ocean. Cornwall was transferred to the south atlantic in late 1939 where she escorted Convoys before returning in 1941 to the indian Ocean. Eventually she sank the german auxiliary Cruiser Pinguin in may. She began escorting Convoys when the pacific War began in December 1941 until she was transferred to the eastern Fleet in March 1942. The Ship was Sink during the indian Ocean Raid on April 5 by dive Bombers from three japanese Aircraft Carriers."
