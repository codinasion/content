short_summary: "Competition between Soviet and Canadian professional ice hockey players"
description: "The Summit Series, Super Series 72, Canada–Ussr Series, or Series of the Century, was an eight-game Ice Hockey Series between the Soviet Union and Canada, held in September 1972. It was the first Competition between the soviet national Team and a canadian Team represented by professional Players from the national Hockey League known as Team Canada. It was Canada's first Ice Hockey Competition after they withdrawn from such Competitions in a Dispute with the international Ice Hockey Federation. The Series was organized with the Intention to create a real Ice Hockey Ice hockey Competition against the best. The Soviets had become the dominant Team in international Competitions in which canadian Professionals were ineligible to play. Canada had a long History of Dominance over this Sport before the Rise of the Soviets."
