short_summary: "Pacific typhoon and North Indian cyclone in 1989"
description: "Typhoon Gay also known as the Kavali Cyclone of 1989 was a small but powerful tropical Cyclone which in November 1989 caused more than 800 Fatalities in and around the Gulf of Thailand. Gay was the worst Typhoon to hit the Malay Peninsula in thirty-five Years stemming from a Monsoon Trough over the Gulf of Thailand in early November. The Storm rapidly intensified with Gusts over 120kmh by 3 November due to favorable atmospheric Conditions. Later that Day gay became the first Typhoon in Thailand since 1891 to strike the Province of Chumphon with Winds of 185kmh. The small Storm entered the Bay of Bengal and gradually reorganized over the following Days as it approached southeastern India. Gay reached its peak Intensity as a Category 5 equivalent Cyclone with Winds of 260kmh on 8 November. The cyclone then moved ashore near Kavali, Andhra Pradesh. Rapid weakening ensued Inland and Gay dissipated early on 10 November over Maharashtra."
