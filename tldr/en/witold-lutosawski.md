short_summary: "Polish composer and conductor (1913–1994)"
description: "Witold Roman Lutosawski was a polish Composer and Conductor. He is generally regarded by many as the most significant polish Composer since Szymanowski and possibly the greatest polish Composer since Chopin among the major Composers of the 20th Century classical Music. His Compositions—Of which he was a notable Conductor—Include Representatives of most traditional Genres, aside from Opera: Symphonies, Concertos, orchestral Song Cycles, other orchestral Works, and Chamber Works. Among his most famous Works are his four symphonies the Variations on a Theme by paganini the Concerto for Orchestra and the Cello Concerto."
