short_summary: "Pacific typhoon in 1973"
description: "In the Philippines typhoon luming known as Nora tied for the fourth-most intense tropical Cyclone in History. Originating from an Area of low Pressure over the western Pacific Nora was first classified as a tropical Depression on 2 October 1973. Tracking generally to the West the System gradually intensified attained Typhoon Status the following Night. After turning northwestward, the typhoon underwent a period of rapid intensification, during which its central pressure decreased by 77 mb in 24 hours. At the End of this Phase, Nora peaked with Winds of 295 Km/H and a Pressure of 875 Mb, making it the most-intense tropical Cyclone on Record at the Time ; however, this Pressure has since been surpassed by Typhoon June, Typhoon Tip and Hurricane Patricia. The Typhoon then weakened and turned northwest as it approached the Philippines. After brushing Luzon on october7 the System then passed South of Taiwan and ultimately came to land on October10 in China. Once on Shore Nora weakened quickly and dissipated the following Day."
