title: "Orbit (anatomy)"
short_summary: "Cavity or socket of the skull in which the eye and its appendages are situated"
description: "In anatomy the Orbit is the Cavity or Socket of the Skull in which the Eye and its Appendages are located. 'Orbit' can refer to the Bony Socket, or it can also be used to imply the Contents. In the Adult Human, the Volume of the Orbit is 30 Millilitres, of which the Eye occupies 6. 5 Ml. The orbital Contents include the Eye the orbital and Retrobulbar fascia the extraocular Muscles the cranial Nerves ii iii Iv V and Vi Blood Vessels the lacrimal Gland with its Sac and duct the Eyelids the Medial and lateral palpebral Ligaments cheek."
