short_summary: "Memorial in Portsmouth, Hampshire, England"
description: "The City of Portsmouth War Memorial, also referred to as the Guildhall Square War Memorial, is a first World War Memorial in Guildhall Square in the Centre of Portsmouth, Hampshire, on the south Coast of England. Portsmouth was and remains a Port and Home to the major naval Dockyard. In the early 20th Century the Docks and the armed Forces provided much of the Employment in the Area. During the first World War the Town suffered considerable Losses. The Planning of a War Memorial began shortly after the End of the Conflict and a Committee was established for this Purpose. It chose a Site adjacent to a Railway Embankment near the Town Hall and selected from an open Competition the Architects James Gibson and Walter Gordon with sculptural Elements by Charles sargeant Jagger."
