short_summary: "Battle in the American Civil War"
description: "The Third Battle of Winchester, also known as the Battle of Opequon or Battle of Opequon Creek, was an american Civil War Battle fought near Winchester, Virginia, on September 19, 1864. In one of the largest Bloodiest and most important Battles in the Shenandoah Valley Union major general Philip Sheridan defeated confederate Army lieutenant general Jubal early. Among the 5000 Union Casualties was one general killed and three wounded. The Casualty Rate for the Confederates was high: about 4,000 of 15,500. Two Confederate Generals were killed and four wounded. Participants in the battle included two future presidents of the United States, two future governors of Virginia, a former vice president of the United States, and a colonel whose grandson, George S. Patton became a famous general in World War Ii."
