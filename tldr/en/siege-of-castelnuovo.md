title: "Siege of Castelnuovo"
short_summary: "Part of the Ottoman–Venetian War (1537–1540)"
description: "The Siege of Castelnuovo was an Engagement during the ottoman - Habsburg Struggle for Control of the Mediterranean which took Place at the walled Town of Castelnuovo Today herceg novi Montenegro in July 1539. Castelnuovo had been conquered by Elements of various spanish Tercios during the failed Campaign of the holy League in eastern mediterranean Waters against the Ottoman empire the Year before. The walled Town was besieged by Land and Sea by a powerful ottoman Army under Hayreddin Barbarossa which offered an honourable Surrender to the Defenders. These Terms were rejected by the spanish commanding Officer Francisco de Sarmiento and his Captains even though they knew that the Holy League Fleet defeated at the Battle of Preveza could not relieve them. During the Siege Barbarossa's Army suffered heavy Losses due to the stubborn Resistance of the Sarmiento Men. However Castelnuovo fell into ottoman Hands and almost all of the spanish Defenders including Sarmiento were killed. The Loss of the Town ended the christian Attempt to regain Control over the eastern Mediterranean. But the Courage displayed by the old Tercio of Naples during this last Stand was praised and admired throughout Europe and was the Subject of many Poems and Songs. Even the Venetian refused the promised naval Link to Castelnuovo with the Supplies and Reinforcements ; after a general Troop Parliament, the commanding Officer Francisco De Sarmiento asked his Captains what his Answer should be to Barbarossa's Offer to surrender. The Captains replied que vengan Cuando Quieran."
