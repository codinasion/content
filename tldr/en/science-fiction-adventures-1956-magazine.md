short_summary: "American digest-size science fiction magazine (1956–58)"
description: "Science Fiction Adventures was an american digest-sized Science Fiction Magazine published by irwin Stein's royal Publications from 1956 to 1958 as a Companion to Infinity Science Fiction which had been published in. Larry Shaw was the Editor of all 12 Issues. These were often labeled as Novels though they were rarely more than 20000 Words long. Shaw declared in his first Editorial that he wanted to bring back a Sense of Wonder and he published straightforward Action-Adventure Stories. Two other Magazines of the Period Imagination and imaginative Tales had similar Editorial Approaches but Science Fiction historian Mike Ashley Considers that science Fiction Adventures contained the best Fiction of the three. Robert Silverberg was a prolific Contributor under his own Name and under the Pseudonym Calvin M. Knox', and he also collaborated with Randall Garrett on two Stories in the first Issue, under two different Pseudonyms. Other prominent Writers have occasionally appeared including Cyril M Ellison Harlan Ellison. Kornbluth, Algis Budrys, and Harry Harrison. Ed Emshwiller contributed cover Art for nine of the Twelve Issues and one of the other three was among john Schoenherr's earliest Sales."
